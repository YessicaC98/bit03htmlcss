Frequency hopping story

In 1941, with half the world at war, he wanted to do his bit. Together with composer George Antheil he developed a system to improve the radio communication of the British submarines, which ended up destroyed before the superiority of the Germans.

How could he make radio communications secure so that submarines would not be intercepted by German torpedoes? It was then that he found the prelude to frequency jumping, a secret communication system to avoid interference and make guided missiles possible. The patent was classified as top secret and was not immediately used.

In 1962 his patent was first used in the Cuban Missile Crisis, and later in Vietnam. It also played a predominant role in the American satellite defense system (Milstar), until in the 1980s, the expanded spectrum system saw its first contributions in civil engineering.

From here, with the irruption of digital technology, the frequency jump allowed to implement data communication by Wifi. It also makes it possible to have a conversation by mobile phone in a room where there is another mobile, connect two devices via Bluetooth or that a mobile receives GPS coordinates from a satellite.
